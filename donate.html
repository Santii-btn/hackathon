A History of Giving in NYC

New York City has long depended on food donations, mutual aid, and community support to help neighbors in need.

Early food challenges

In the early 1900s, New York faced rising food prices and supply issues, showing how quickly the city could be affected by shortages.

Community response

Volunteers, food pantries, and nonprofit groups now help distribute meals and groceries across the city every day.

Today’s need

Food insecurity remains a major issue in NYC, with city reports showing about 1.2 million residents food insecure and some neighborhoods facing much higher rates.
